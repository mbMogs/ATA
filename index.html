Memories of ATA Airlines

American Trans Air
Class 89-12

My childhood dream was to travel the world and experience the cultures
from around the globe. In December of 1989, I received an invitation
to attend flight attendant training with ATA Airlines. After 4 weeks of
training, I earned my wings and I became FA #94999. I couldn't wait
for my adventures to begin!

The 1990's

My first base was at Chicago O'Hare International
Airport (ORD). I worked some fun filled charters
to many cities, including London, Paris, Rome,
and Cairo. Desert Shield/Desert Storm started and I had
the privilege of flying the troops around during the Gulf war.
I remember looking down at the Persian Gulf and seeing
the warships lined up in the waters. It was quite a sight
to behold. I also worked a trip to Somalia to evacuate the
US Embassy, which had come under attack during civil unrest.

Hawaii, Washington D.C. and Seattle were my favorite domestic layovers.

I was in London the week Princess Diana was killed in a car accident.
I placed flowers near her memorial at Kensington Palace.
It was a very sad time in England.

The 2000's

I enjoyed working luxury "around the world" trips on the L1011.
My South America trip included layovers in Mexico, Ecuador,
Easter Island, Machu Picchu, Iguazu Falls, Argentina and Brazil.
Experiencing the cultures of these places was such a thrill.

I also worked a trip which visited Japan,
Thailand, India, Greece, Crete, and Spain.
Touring the cities and experiencing the cultures
at each stop was an amazing experience.

Once the Iraq War started, most of my trips
involved bringing the troops back and forth
from the war. Working with the military was
one of the highlights of my aviation career.

On April 3, 2008, ATA Airlines closed its doors for good.
My ATA adventures and the many friends that I made
will always be a treasured part of my life.
